Facts

  • The moon doesn't actually have a permanent "dark side".
    Both sides gets the same amount of sunlight its just that only one side is seen from Earth.
  • It needs space.
    The moon drifts away from the Earth at a whopping 3.8 cm a year and will continue to do so for 50 billions of years.
  • Low gravity
    The moon's gravitational force about a sixth of Earths meaning you would weigh around 15% of your Earth bodyweight.
  • If a tree falls...
    The moon has no atmosphere so no sound can be heard no matter whose around. This also means the moon is susceptle to the cosmic rays and solar winds Earths atmosphere shields us from.

What's the moon smell like?

Apparently some astronauts experienced a gunpowder aroma when smelling the Moon's dust. This could be from the oxidation that would be too slow to create smoke but enough to create the aroma.

How big is the moon?

The Moon's diameter is about 2,200 miles, which compared to earth makes it about less than a third of the size of Earth.

Whats the temperature of the Moon?

The temperature on the surface can reach 260F° when the Sun is out and at night can plummet to around 280F°.

  • Origin Story
    The Moon is thought to be a piece of Earth that was separated after a rock slammed into Earth about 4.5 billion years ago.
  • Distance
    The Moon is about 250,000 miles away from Earth. To travel by car at 60 mph would take about 6 months so hopefully there's at least a Kum and Go somewhere.
  • Orbit
    The Moon orbits Earth once every 27 days.
